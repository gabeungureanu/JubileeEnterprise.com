Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Jubilee Browser.

General

What is Jubilee Browser?

Jubilee Browser is a dual-mode web browser designed for the faith community. It allows you to browse the regular internet while also providing access to the Jubilee Bible Internet through the inspire:// protocol. It combines safe browsing, Scripture access, and content filtering in a single, trusted application.

Is Jubilee Browser free?

Yes, Jubilee Browser is completely free to download and use for personal, church, and school use. There are no hidden fees, subscriptions, or premium versions. We believe technology that serves the Church should be accessible to everyone.

What makes Jubilee Browser different from other browsers?

Jubilee Browser provides a clear separation between the public internet and a Scripture-centered digital environment. It includes built-in content filtering that cannot be bypassed, direct access to Bible resources, and support for church and school deployment. Unlike traditional browsers, Scripture is at the center of the experience, not just another website.

What platforms are supported?

Currently, Jubilee Browser is available for Windows 10 and Windows 11 (64-bit). macOS and Linux versions are planned for future releases.

What is the inspire:// protocol?

The inspire:// protocol is a custom URL scheme that provides direct access to the Jubilee Bible Internet - a dedicated, faith-based digital network. When you click an inspire:// link or switch to Jubilee Bibles mode, you enter a Scripture-centered environment without ads, data harvesting, or secular content.

Safety & Protection

How does Jubilee Browser protect my family?

Jubilee Browser addresses one of the most persistent problems in Christian households: exposure to pornography and inappropriate content. By design, unsafe sites are blocked and cannot be overridden by user settings. This protects children, teens, and adults alike without requiring parents to manage complex filtering tools.

Can users disable the content filtering?

No. The content filtering is built into the browser by design and cannot be disabled or bypassed by users. This is intentional - it removes the burden from parents, pastors, and IT teams who often struggle to maintain filtering across multiple devices.

Does Jubilee Browser collect my browsing data?

No. Jubilee Browser does not collect or transmit your browsing history, search queries, bookmarks, or personal data. Your browsing stays on your computer. We only collect minimal information for update checks (version number and OS) and optional crash reports.

Browsing Modes

What's the difference between Internet and Jubilee Bibles mode?

Internet mode works like a regular web browser for browsing the public web (with content filtering active). Jubilee Bibles mode is designed for accessing inspire:// content - Scripture, devotionals, and faith-based resources in a distraction-free environment. Each mode has separate cookies, history, and storage for privacy.

Why have two separate modes?

The separation creates a visible and intentional boundary between general web browsing and Scripture-centered activity. For churches and families, this reinforces the idea that time spent in God's Word is intentional and set apart. It also keeps your faith-based browsing separate from regular internet activity.

Installation

Which installer should I download?

For most users, we recommend the Web Installer (~2 MB). It downloads the latest version during installation. If you have a slow internet connection or need to install on computers without internet access (or for church/school deployment), use the Offline Installer (~80 MB).

Windows SmartScreen is blocking the installer. Is it safe?

Yes, the installer is safe. Windows SmartScreen shows warnings for new software that hasn't built up reputation yet. Click "More info" and then "Run anyway" to proceed. The installer is digitally signed by Jubilee Software, Inc.

Do I need administrator rights to install?

No, by default Jubilee Browser installs per-user and doesn't require administrator rights. If you want to install for all users on a computer (common for churches and schools), you'll need admin rights and should use the /ALLUSERS option.

Can I install silently for church or school deployment?

Yes! Use JubileeSetup.exe /S for silent installation. Add /ALLUSERS for per-machine installation. See our Churches & Schools page for full deployment documentation.

Updates

How do automatic updates work?

Jubilee Browser checks for updates every 4 hours in the background. When an update is available, it downloads automatically. The update is installed when you restart the browser. Your tabs, bookmarks, and settings are preserved across updates.

Can I disable automatic updates?

Yes, automatic updates can be disabled in Settings. For church and school deployments, you can disable updates using a policy file to control updates centrally and deploy new versions on your own schedule.

Churches & Schools

How can our church use Jubilee Browser?

Jubilee Browser is perfect for church offices, Sunday school rooms, libraries, and any shared computers. It protects against inappropriate content by design, provides easy access to Scripture, and can be deployed silently across all computers. Many churches also recommend it to families in their congregation for home use.

Is it suitable for Christian schools?

Absolutely. Jubilee Browser simplifies the challenge of keeping students safe online. Deploy it across classrooms and labs with silent installation, configure default settings and locked policies, and let automatic updates keep everything current without manual maintenance.

Do you offer support for organizational deployment?

Yes! We're happy to help churches and schools deploy Jubilee Browser. Visit our Churches & Schools page for documentation, or contact us for personalized assistance.

Troubleshooting

Jubilee Browser won't start. What should I do?

Try running the browser from the command line to see error messages. If the issue persists, try renaming the user data folder at %LOCALAPPDATA%\JubileeBrowser to reset settings. See our Troubleshooting Guide for more help.

Pages are loading slowly. How can I speed things up?

Try clearing your cache in Settings > Privacy > Clear Browsing Data. Make sure hardware acceleration is enabled in Settings > Advanced. Close unused tabs to free up memory.

Where are my settings and data stored?

User data is stored in %LOCALAPPDATA%\JubileeBrowser. This includes settings, bookmarks, history, and session data. This folder is preserved during updates and uninstallation (unless you choose to remove it).
